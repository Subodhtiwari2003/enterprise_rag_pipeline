Revenue Recognition Policy – Enterprise Finance Guidelines

1. SCOPE
This policy applies to all revenue-generating activities of the organisation
and is aligned with IFRS 15 (Revenue from Contracts with Customers).

2. FIVE-STEP MODEL
The organisation follows the IFRS 15 five-step revenue recognition model:
  Step 1 – Identify the contract with a customer.
  Step 2 – Identify the performance obligations in the contract.
  Step 3 – Determine the transaction price.
  Step 4 – Allocate the transaction price to the performance obligations.
  Step 5 – Recognise revenue when (or as) the entity satisfies a performance obligation.

3. TIMING
Revenue is recognised at the point in time when control of goods or services
is transferred to the customer. For long-term contracts (>12 months), revenue
is recognised over time using the percentage-of-completion method.

4. VARIABLE CONSIDERATION
Variable consideration (e.g. discounts, rebates, penalties) is estimated using
the expected-value method and included in the transaction price only to the extent
that it is highly probable that a significant revenue reversal will not occur.

5. CONTRACT MODIFICATIONS
Modifications to existing contracts are treated as either:
  (a) A separate contract – if distinct goods/services are added at standalone price.
  (b) A modification to the existing contract – if goods/services are not distinct.

6. REPORTING
Monthly revenue reports are submitted to the CFO by the 5th of each following month.
Quarterly reports are reviewed by the Audit Committee.

7. APPROVAL THRESHOLDS
  • Revenue adjustments < ₹1 Lakh: Finance Manager approval.
  • Revenue adjustments ₹1L – ₹10L: CFO approval.
  • Revenue adjustments > ₹10L: Board Finance Committee approval.
""".strip()

Financial Risk Management Framework

4. LIQUIDITY MANAGEMENT
  • Minimum cash buffer: 3 months of operating expenses maintained at all times.
